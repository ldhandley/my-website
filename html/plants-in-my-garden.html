Lilac

The lilac is a beloved shrub known for its fragrant spring blooms and classic cottage garden charm. Though not native to the Pacific Northwest, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, rewarding gardeners each year with clusters of purple, pink, or white flowers that attract pollinators and nostalgic gardeners alike. A mature lilac can become a centerpiece in your garden, providing both beauty and scent as it bursts into bloom each spring.

Menzie's Larkspur

Menzies’ Larkspur is a striking native wildflower found in prairies and open woods throughout the Puget Sound region. With its vibrant blue-violet spurred flowers and deeply lobed leaves, this plant adds a burst of color to early spring gardens. It prefers well-drained soils and sunny to lightly shaded spots, and it’s a favorite of native bumblebees and butterflies. This species is a great way to bring more biodiversity into your landscape while honoring the beauty of local ecosystems.

Hydrangea

Hydrangeas are a classic garden staple known for their lush, globe-like flower heads and ability to shift color depending on soil pH. While not native to the Pacific Northwest, they grow wonderfully in the region’s mild, moist climate and make a lovely addition to shaded garden areas. With varieties ranging from bold blues to delicate pinks and creamy whites, hydrangeas offer long-lasting blooms from late spring through summer and provide structural interest even in the dormant months.
